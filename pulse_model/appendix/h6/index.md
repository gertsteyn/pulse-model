# H6 Decoherence And Source Response

H6 asks whether classical spacetime can be understood as the structure left after alternative pulse histories decohere. H6S1 asks how a quantum source might source a gravitational response in a weak-field diagnostic setup. H6S2 asks whether a candidate response may depend on an arbitrary ensemble decomposition of the same local source density operator. H6S3 proves that branch-dependent probe variance is not physical without records, a stochastic classical pulse-geometry law, or a non-classical geometry or mediator route.

## Current Verdict

H6 is accepted only as bounded branch-decoherence bookkeeping and model comparison. H6S1 is a useful executable quantum source-response diagnostic. H6S2 is a diagnostic ensemble-decomposition-invariance admissibility filter with a clean no-go subtheorem for unsupported spacelike branch response. H6S3 is a diagnostic no-free-branch-variance theorem and route ledger. None of these supplies a full classical-spacetime emergence theorem or a physical source-response law.

## Rejected Overclaims

- no accepted source-response law
- no full emergence of classical spacetime
- no permission to treat expectation-value sourcing, branch selection, or collapse placeholders as solved
- no permission to treat branch-mixture variance as physical without local records, causal selection, or shared-future comparison
- no permission to treat chosen ensemble branch variance as physical without records, stochastic classical pulse geometry, or non-classical geometry or mediator support
- no controllable faster-than-light signaling rule
